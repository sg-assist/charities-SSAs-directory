## What This Card Is For

This card lists warning signs during pregnancy. If you see or hear about any of these signs, act right away. Do not wait.

---

## REFER IMMEDIATELY — Do Not Wait

Call for transport or emergency help **now** if the woman has any of these signs.

| Warning Sign | What It Looks Like |
|---|---|
| Heavy bleeding from the vagina | Soaking more than one pad per hour, or any bleeding with clots |
| Fits or convulsions | Shaking she cannot control, body goes stiff, she loses awareness |
| Severe headache | Very bad headache that does not go away with rest |
| Vision changes | Blurry vision, seeing spots or flashing lights, or sudden loss of sight |
| Difficulty breathing | Breathing very fast, cannot finish a sentence, lips or fingers look blue |
| Unconscious or very confused | She cannot wake up or does not know where she is |

---

## REFER SAME DAY — Go Today

The woman should go to a health facility today — do not wait until tomorrow.

| Warning Sign | What It Looks Like |
|---|---|
| Fever | Body feels very hot, shivering, temperature above 38°C (100.4°F) |
| No baby movement | Baby has not moved for 12 hours or more (after 28 weeks) |
| Swollen face, hands, or feet | Puffiness that came on quickly, especially in the morning |
| Painful or burning when passing urine | Could be a urine infection |
| Leaking fluid from the vagina (not urine) | Water leaking before labour is due |
| Severe belly pain | Constant, strong pain that does not come and go like labour |

---

## How to Check for Swelling

1. Press your thumb on the woman's lower leg for 3 seconds.
2. Remove your thumb.
3. If a dent stays in the skin, that is a danger sign — refer same day.

---

## What to Say to the Woman

- "I want to make sure you and your baby are safe. I am taking you to the clinic."
- Stay calm. Do not frighten her.
- Stay with her until she reaches care if you can.

---

## Quick Checklist — Ask at Every Visit

- [ ] Any bleeding?
- [ ] Any headache or vision problems?
- [ ] Any swelling of face or hands?
- [ ] Is the baby moving?
- [ ] Any fever?
- [ ] Any fits or fainting?
- [ ] Any pain or leaking fluid?

If the answer is **yes** to any question — refer based on the table above.

---

## When in Doubt: Refer

If you are unsure, always refer to the nearest health facility. It is never wrong to ask for help.
