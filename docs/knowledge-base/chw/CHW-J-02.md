## What This Card Is For

This card lists warning signs in a newborn baby (first 28 days of life). Any of these signs means the baby needs care right away. Do not wait.

---

## REFER IMMEDIATELY — Do Not Wait

Get the baby to a health facility **now** if you see any of these signs.

| Warning Sign | What to Look or Listen For |
|---|---|
| Not breathing or gasping | Chest is not rising, no crying, skin turning blue or grey |
| Fits or convulsions | Body shaking or stiffening the baby cannot control, eyes rolling |
| Unconscious or cannot wake up | Baby does not respond to touch or sound |
| Severe difficulty breathing | Breathing very fast (more than 60 breaths per minute) or grunting with each breath |
| Bulging soft spot (fontanelle) | The soft area on top of the head is pushed outward — especially with fever |
| Cold baby who cannot be warmed | Skin feels cold to touch after 30 minutes of skin-to-skin or wrapping |

---

## REFER SAME DAY — Go Today

Take the baby to a health facility today if you see any of these signs.

| Warning Sign | What to Look or Listen For |
|---|---|
| Not feeding at all | Baby has not fed for 8 hours or more, or is too weak to suck |
| Yellow skin or yellow eyes (jaundice) | Yellowing appears on day 1, or spreads to the belly, arms, or legs |
| Fever | Baby feels hot, temperature above 37.5°C (99.5°F) |
| Umbilical cord looks infected | Cord stump is red, swollen, or has pus — redness spreading onto the belly skin |
| Redness or pus in the eyes | Eyes stuck together or with yellow-green discharge |
| Many skin blisters or sores | Blisters or pus-filled sores spreading across the body |

---

## How to Keep the Baby Warm While Referring

1. Keep the baby skin-to-skin with the mother inside her clothing.
2. Cover both mother and baby with a blanket.
3. Keep the baby's head covered.
4. Do not let the baby lie alone on a cold surface.

---

## How to Count Breathing

1. Watch the baby's chest for 1 full minute.
2. Count each time the chest rises — that is one breath.
3. More than 60 breaths per minute = danger sign, refer immediately.

---

## What to Say to the Mother

- "Your baby needs to be checked by a nurse or doctor today."
- Stay calm. Do not alarm her more than needed.
- Help arrange transport if possible.

---

## Quick Checklist — Check at Every Home Visit

- [ ] Is the baby breathing normally?
- [ ] Is the baby feeding well?
- [ ] Any yellow colour in skin or eyes?
- [ ] Does the baby feel hot or cold?
- [ ] Does the cord stump look clean?
- [ ] Any fits or unusual movements?
- [ ] Is the soft spot on top of the head flat and soft?

If the answer shows a problem — refer based on the tables above.

---

## When in Doubt: Refer

If you are unsure, always refer to the nearest health facility. It is never wrong to ask for help.
