## What This Card Is For

This card helps you support someone who may have experienced gender-based violence (GBV). GBV is any act that harms a person because of their gender. It includes physical violence, sexual violence, emotional harm, and forced control.

Your role is to **listen, support, and refer** — not to investigate or solve the problem yourself.

---

## Step 1 — Create a Safe Space

Before you begin:

- [ ] Find a private place where no one else can hear the conversation.
- [ ] Make sure the person who caused harm is not nearby.
- [ ] Speak quietly and stay calm.
- [ ] Put away any paper or device that others could see.
- [ ] Ask: "Is it safe for us to talk here right now?"

---

## Step 2 — What to Say

**Say these things:**

- "I believe you."
- "This is not your fault."
- "Thank you for telling me. That took courage."
- "You have the right to feel safe."
- "I am here to help you find support."

**Do not say these things:**

- "Why did you stay?" or "Why did you not leave?"
- "Are you sure this happened?"
- "What did you do to cause this?"
- "You should forgive him / work it out."
- "This is a family matter."

---

## Step 3 — Assess Immediate Safety

Ask gently:

1. "Are you safe right now?"
2. "Is the person who hurt you nearby or at home?"
3. "Do you have children who may also be at risk?"

| Situation | Action |
|---|---|
| She is in danger right now | Call police or emergency services immediately. Do not leave her alone. |
| She is safe for now but fears returning home | Help her identify a safe place to go. Offer to connect her to a shelter. |
| She feels safe at home | Continue with support and referral options below. |

---

## Step 4 — Documentation (Only if Safe)

Write notes **only if it is safe** to do so — notes that the person who caused harm finds could put her in more danger.

If it is safe to document, record:
- Date and time you spoke
- What the person told you in her own words (do not add your opinion)
- Visible injuries — describe what you see, do not photograph without permission
- What referrals were offered and accepted

Keep notes locked away or in a secure system. Do not share with anyone outside the care team without consent.

---

## Step 5 — Referral Options

Offer these options. Let the survivor choose. Do not pressure her.

| Type of Support | Who to Contact |
|---|---|
| Medical care (injury, sexual assault, health check) | Nearest polyclinic, hospital emergency, or family planning clinic |
| Counselling and emotional support | Family Service Centre (FSC) in her area |
| Safety shelter / refuge | PAVE, AWARE, or TRANS SAFE Centre (Singapore) |
| Legal advice | Legal Aid Bureau or Pro Bono SG |
| Police report | Singapore Police Force — she can report at any police post |
| Child protection (if children are at risk) | Ministry of Social and Family Development (MSF) Child Protective Service: 1800-777-0000 |

**Key helplines — Singapore:**

| Service | Number |
|---|---|
| National Anti-Violence & Sexual Harassment Helpline | 1800-777-0000 |
| AWARE Women's Helpline | 1800-777-5555 |
| PAVE (family violence specialist centre) | 6555-0390 |
| TRANS SAFE Centre | 6449-9088 |
| Samaritans of Singapore (crisis) | 1800-221-4444 |
| Police / Emergency | 999 |

---

## Step 6 — Safety Planning Basics

Help her think through these questions:

1. **Safe place:** "Where can you go if you need to leave quickly? A friend, family member, or shelter?"
2. **Important items:** "Do you have documents like your ID card, children's cards, or some cash you can keep somewhere safe?"
3. **Code word:** "Can you agree on a word with someone you trust — so they know to call for help when you say it?"
4. **Emergency contacts:** "Who are two people you can call day or night if you are in danger?"

Write nothing down unless she agrees and it is safe to do so.

---

## Mandatory Reporting — Singapore Context

In Singapore, there are situations where you **must** report — even without the survivor's consent:

- **A child (under 18) is being abused or is at risk** — report to MSF Child Protective Service: 1800-777-0000
- **Someone's life is in immediate danger** — call Police: 999

For adults, reporting is generally the survivor's choice. Encourage her, but do not force her. Explain what reporting involves so she can decide.

If you are unsure whether reporting is required, speak to your supervisor or case manager.

---

## After the Conversation

- [ ] Follow up with the survivor at the next safe opportunity.
- [ ] Do not share her story with others in the community.
- [ ] Look after yourself — these conversations can be hard. Talk to your supervisor if you need support.

---

## When in Doubt: Refer

If you are unsure, always refer to the nearest health facility. It is never wrong to ask for help.
