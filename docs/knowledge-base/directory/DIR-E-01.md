## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Singapore's eldercare services are coordinated through the Agency for Integrated Care (AIC). As of 2024, there are approximately 88 nursing home locations (~48 VWO-run, ~40 private) with ~19,200 beds, 20+ senior care centre operators with 100+ locations, and numerous home care providers. This document lists every major provider with individual branch addresses and contact details.

**AIC Hotline: 1800-650-6060** (Mon–Fri 8:30am–8:30pm, Sat 8:30am–4pm) | www.aic.sg

## CAREGIVER SUPPORT

### Home Caregiving Grant (HCG)

- **Amount:** $200–$600/month (means-tested). Maximum $600 from April 2026.
- **Eligibility:** Care recipient is SG Citizen/PR, living with caregiver, not institutionalised, needs help with 3+ ADLs. PCHI ≤$4,800.
- **Apply:** Doctor/nurse signs Functional Assessment Report → submit to AIC. Processing 6–8 weeks.
- Info: www.aic.sg/financial-assistance/home-caregiving-grant-hcg/

### Caregivers Training Grant (CTG)

- **Amount:** $400 first year, $200 annual top-up
- **Apply:** Inform trainer 2 weeks before course; trainer provides CTG form
- Info: www.aic.sg/financial-assistance/caregivers-training-grant-ctg/

### Respite Care

- **Senior Care Centre Respite** — short-term day care for ad hoc breaks
- **Nursing Home Respite** — short-term residential stay (up to 30 days)
- **Home-Based Respite** — trained staff visit the home
- **Dementia Home Support** — staff engage seniors in mind-stimulating activities

## SUBSIDIES

### Nursing Home Subsidies (Residential LTC)

| PCHI | Citizens | PRs |
|------|----------|-----|
| ≤$900 | 75% | 50% |
| $901–$1,500 | 60% | — |
| $1,501–$2,300 | 50% | — |
| $2,301–$2,600 | 40% | — |

From July 2026: up to **80%** for citizens born in/before 1969.

### Day Care / Home Care Subsidies (Non-Residential LTC)

- Citizens: up to **80%** (up to **95%** from July 2026 for those born in/before 1969)
- PRs: up to 55%

### Seniors' Mobility and Enabling Fund (SMF)

- Up to **90% subsidy** on assistive devices (wheelchairs, walking aids, hospital beds, hearing aids)
- Eligibility: Citizens 60+, PCHI ≤$2,000

### ElderFund

- Monthly cash payout up to **$250** for severely disabled, lower-income Singaporeans 30+
- Eligibility: Cannot perform 3+ ADLs; PCHI ≤$1,200
- Apply via AIC eFASS portal

### Other Financial Support

- **CareShield Life** — national long-term care insurance (born 1980+); monthly payouts upon severe disability
- **ElderShield** — predecessor; $300–$400/month for 60–72 months
- **MediFund** — safety net for remaining hospital bills
- **Pioneer/Merdeka Generation** — additional subsidies for older Singaporeans

## HOW TO ACCESS SERVICES

1. **Contact AIC** (1800-650-6060) — central coordination for all eldercare services
2. **Visit AIC Link centres** — walk-in information and assistance
3. **For nursing home admission** — approach Medical Social Worker at hospital/polyclinic → MSW submits to AIC → AIC manages placement
4. **For home care/day care** — referral through AIC or direct contact with provider
5. **For subsidies** — means-tested, applied through AIC or the service provider
