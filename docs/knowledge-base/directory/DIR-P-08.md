*If you have just lost your income, had an unexpected bill, or don't know how you'll cover the basics this month — this guide tells you exactly where to go and in what order. Singapore's safety net exists. Here is how to use it.*

---

## THE MOST IMPORTANT THING TO KNOW

**Call ComCare at 1800-222-0000** (free, 24 hours). This is the front door to Singapore's main financial assistance system. Or walk into any **Social Service Office (SSO)** — no appointment needed.

While you are sorting out medium-term help, your **CDC (Community Development Council)** can also give emergency assistance quickly. And there are food resources available today, with no paperwork.

---

## 1. FIRST 48 HOURS — IMMEDIATE FOOD AND CASH RELIEF

Before forms and assessments: here is what you can access today or tomorrow, with little or no paperwork.

### Food banks and meal programmes

| Organisation | What they provide | Contact |
|-------------|-------------------|---------|
| **Food from the Heart** | Monthly grocery packs, bread runs, school meals | 6280 4483 / foodfromtheheart.sg |
| **The Food Bank Singapore** | Emergency food hampers via referral from FSCs and charities | 6542 3361 / foodbank.sg |
| **Willing Hearts** | Free daily meals, delivery available | 6441 6468 / willinghearts.org.sg |
| **Fei Yue Community Services** | Food assistance, vouchers | 6873 0030 |
| **NTUC FairPrice Community Care Fund** | Grocery vouchers | Via FSC or SSO |

**You do not need a referral to walk in or call most food banks.** The Food Bank Singapore typically requires a referral from a social service agency — go to your FSC or SSO and ask them to refer you.

### CDC Emergency Assistance

Your district CDC (there are five: Central Singapore, North East, North West, South East, South West) can provide emergency cash assistance, grocery vouchers, or utility assistance.

- **Find your CDC and hotline:** cdc.org.sg
- Each CDC has staff who can assess and disburse emergency help quickly — sometimes within the same week
- CDC Vouchers worth **$800 (FY2025/2026)** are also available to every household regardless of income: **go.gov.sg/cdcv**

### Community emergency funds

Many FSCs and religious organisations keep small emergency funds for food, transport, or bills. Call your nearest FSC (go.gov.sg/fsc) and explain your situation — even if you cannot come in that day.

## 2. APPLYING FOR COMCARE — THE MAIN SAFETY NET

ComCare is the government's primary financial assistance programme, run by the Ministry of Social and Family Development (MSF).

### The two main schemes for working-age adults

**Immediate Financial Assistance (also called ComCare Interim Assistance)**
- For acute, immediate crises — no food, bills overdue, just lost a job
- Duration: up to 3 months while longer-term is arranged
- Can be approved quickly, sometimes on the day of the first visit
- Form: cash, supermarket vouchers, food rations

**Short-to-Medium-Term Assistance (SMTA)**
- For people temporarily unable to work, between jobs, or earning too little to cover basic needs
- Duration: 3–6 months per cycle, renewable
- **Income guideline:** Per Capita Household Income (PCHI) ≤ **$800/month**
- Typical cash payout: ~$400–$800+/month, plus add-ons (utilities, rental rebates, school fees)

The SSO officer decides which scheme fits you — you do not need to know which one to ask for.

### Which Family Service Centre or SSO to go to

**SSOs** are the primary channel for ComCare applications. Go to the SSO serving **your residential address**.

There are 24 SSOs across Singapore (Ang Mo Kio, Bedok, Boon Lay, Bukit Batok, Bukit Merah, Bukit Panjang, Chua Chu Kang, Clementi, Geylang Serai, Hougang, Jalan Besar, Jurong East, Kreta Ayer, Pasir Ris, Punggol, Queenstown, Sembawang, Sengkang, Serangoon, Taman Jurong, Tampines, Toa Payoh, Woodlands, Yishun).

**Hours:** Monday–Friday 8:30am–6pm. No appointment needed for a first visit. Go in the morning.

**Family Service Centres (FSCs)** can also help with the application and provide counselling alongside. Find yours at go.gov.sg/fsc.

### What documents to bring

Bring what you have. If you don't have something, go anyway — the case can still be opened.

**For every adult in the household:**
- NRIC (original and photocopy)
- Last 3 months' bank statements (every account)
- Last 3 months' payslips, or CPF contribution history (from cpf.gov.sg)
- Termination letter or resignation letter if recently unemployed

**For the household:**
- Tenancy agreement or HDB mortgage statement
- Latest SP Services utility bill
- Any overdue bills, debt letters, medical bills

**If relevant:**
- Medical certificates or hospital letters (medical issues expand eligibility significantly)
- Birth certificates for children
- Children's school fee bills

### What to say at the SSO

Describe your situation plainly — what happened (job loss, pay cut, illness, separation), how long it has been, who is in your household, and what bills you cannot pay. **Tell the full story.** Details about medical conditions, family tensions, and debts all affect the assessment. Officers assess holistically — they are not trying to catch you out.

Ask at the end: *"What other schemes can I apply for today?"*

## 3. UTILITIES AND HOUSING

### Utility bills — SP Group UCARE

If you cannot pay your electricity or gas bill, contact SP Group before the account is terminated.

- **SP Group UCARE Fund** provides assistance for households in genuine financial difficulty
- **SP Group Customer Service: 1800-222-2333** (24 hours)
- Also ask about an instalment plan for overdue amounts — SP Group can hold off disconnection while a plan is in place
- ComCare recipients automatically receive **U-Save rebates** (quarterly utility credits)

### PUB (water bills)

- **PUB Payment Assistance**: PUB has a hardship assistance scheme for those unable to pay water bills
- **PUB Contact: 1800-284-6600**

### HDB rental arrears

If you are renting an HDB flat (public rental or market rental) and falling behind:

- Contact your **HDB Branch** before the debt grows. HDB has a structured arrears management process and will work out a repayment plan
- **HDB Hotline: 1800-225-5432**
- ComCare recipients may be eligible for rental waivers or rebates through the SSO

### Town Council Service and Conservancy Charges (S&CC)

Residents in financial hardship can apply for S&CC rebates or waivers directly to their **Town Council**. ComCare recipients are often automatically enrolled.

## 4. MEDICAL BILLS

Medical bills can quickly become catastrophic. There are multiple levels of help.

### MediFund — the safety net for hospital bills

MediFund is a government endowment that pays residual hospital bills (after subsidies, MediShield Life, and MediSave) for Singapore Citizens who genuinely cannot afford to pay.

- **Apply at the hospital's Medical Social Worker (MSW) office** — not at a counter, at the MSW department
- You can apply for a bill you are already holding, or for a current admission
- The MSW will assess your situation holistically
- MediFund Silver (for those 65+) and MediFund Junior (for children) follow the same process
- There is no formal income cut-off — eligibility is based on ability to pay after all other help

**At every restructured hospital visit**, if you are having difficulty paying, tell the billing counter: *"I need to speak to a Medical Social Worker."*

### CHAS — subsidised GP and dental care

CHAS (Community Health Assist Scheme) gives subsidies on GP and dental visits at participating private clinics.

- **Blue CHAS card:** household PCHI ≤ ~$1,100 — largest subsidies
- **Orange CHAS card:** PCHI ≤ ~$2,300 — standard subsidies
- Covers consultations, basic investigations, and medications for many chronic conditions
- **Enrol via:** go.gov.sg/chas or at your polyclinic

If you do not have a CHAS card, polyclinics are still subsidised and significantly cheaper than private GPs (consultation from ~$5–$14 for citizens).

### Medication Assistance Fund (MAF)

If you need long-term medication that is expensive, MAF provides additional subsidies for specific drugs at public health institutions.

- Assessed by the prescribing doctor or hospital pharmacist
- Applies to medications on the MAF drug list (chronic disease medications, some cancer drugs)
- Ask the hospital's MSW or your polyclinic doctor: *"Am I eligible for MAF for this medication?"*

## 5. CHILDREN'S SCHOOL COSTS

### MOE Financial Assistance Scheme (FAS)

The main scheme for school-age children (primary through pre-university). Provides:
- **Full school fee waiver**
- Free textbooks and school uniform vouchers
- Daily meal subsidy ($2.50/day)
- Annual transport subsidy ($17/month)

**Eligibility:** Gross Household Income (GHI) ≤ **$3,000/month** or Per Capita Income (PCI) ≤ **$750/month** (Singapore Citizens only; PR children may access lower-tier subsidies)

**Apply:** at your child's school General Office at any time of year. The school's admin staff handles it. Bring income documents and NRIC.

### MOE Opportunity Fund

Provides additional help with school-related costs (enrichment, activity fees, camp fees) for students on FAS. Schools administer this fund — ask the school's counsellor or admin.

### Bursaries

Many schools, junior colleges, polytechnics, and ITE campuses have their own bursary funds from donations and government matching.

- Ask the financial aid office at your child's institution
- Also check **SkillsFuture Study Award** for students in vocational pathways

## 6. EMPLOYMENT SUPPORT

Income loss from job loss is different from persistent low income — the pathway out often involves employment support.

### Workforce Singapore (WSG) — job matching

WSG operates career centres across Singapore and online.

- **WSG Career Connect Hotline: 6883 5885**
- Walk-in career centres at: Devan Nair Institute (Jurong), one-stop career centres in various CDCs
- Free career coaching, resume workshops, interview preparation
- Sector-specific programmes for workers displaced from particular industries

### Jobs Growth Incentive and sector-specific hiring incentives

WSG and various agencies periodically run hiring subsidies for employers taking on mid-career workers. Your career coach will know current schemes.

### SkillsFuture

- **SkillsFuture Credit:** all Singapore Citizens 25+ receive credit (currently $500 topped up with $4,000 SkillsFuture Level-Up from age 40+). Use at approved training providers for courses
- **SkillsFuture Jobseeker Support Scheme:** provides monthly financial support for involuntarily unemployed Singaporeans actively looking for work — up to $6,000 over 6 months. Apply at WSG Career Connect centres
- **SkillsFuture website: skillsfuture.gov.sg**

### CDC Job Fairs and Employment Assistance

Your district CDC often runs job fairs and can connect you to local employers and training opportunities. Check cdc.org.sg for your district's events.

### e2i (Employment and Employability Institute)

Provides employment assistance, training, and career coaching, especially for older workers and those transitioning careers.

- **Hotline: 6474 0606**
- **Website:** e2i.com.sg

## 7. CDC VOUCHERS AND COMMUNITY SUPPORT

### CDC Vouchers — $800 for every household

The government issues CDC Vouchers to every Singaporean household (no means test required). For FY2025/2026, each household receives **$800** usable at hawker centres, wet markets, HDB heartland shops, and supermarkets.

- **Claim at:** go.gov.sg/cdcv using your Singpass
- Vouchers expire — claim early and use them before the validity period ends

### Self-Help Groups

Singapore's four Self-Help Groups (SHGs) provide education bursaries, financial assistance, and community support targeted to their respective communities.

| Group | Community | Contact |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| **CDAC** | Chinese | 1800-259-3388 |
| **MENDAKI** | Malay/Muslim | 6245 5700 |
| **SINDA** | Indian | 1800-295-3333 |
| **Eurasian Association** | Eurasian | 6475 3882 |

Your SHG may have emergency financial assistance, education bursaries, or welfare support that ComCare does not cover. Call them even if you are also applying for ComCare.

### Religious organisations

Many mosques, temples, and churches have welfare funds for emergency assistance. If you are affiliated with a religious community, contact them — many do not require proof of membership to help.

## THE MOST IMPORTANT SINGLE SENTENCE

**Go to the SSO early, bring what documents you have, and tell the officer everything — the more they know about your situation, the more help they can give you.**

Singapore's safety net is designed to be used. Using it when you need it is exactly what it is there for.
