*If you're reading this because you're worried about rent, food, or bills — take a breath. Singapore has a safety net called ComCare, administered by MSF. This guide walks you from "I need help" to "money in my bank account."*

## THE MOST IMPORTANT THING TO KNOW

**Call the ComCare Hotline: 1800-222-0000** (free, 24 hours). Or walk into any Social Service Office (SSO) during office hours. You do not need an appointment for a first visit. You do not need to know which scheme to apply for — the officer will figure that out. The income thresholds are **guidelines, not hard walls**. Even if you "earn too much" on paper, still go. Assessments are holistic.

---

## 1. THE FOUR TYPES OF COMCARE

ComCare is an umbrella over four schemes. The SSO officer decides which one fits — you do not pick.

### A. ComCare Short-to-Medium-Term Assistance (SMTA)

For people temporarily unable to work, looking for a job, or earning too little.

- **Who:** Singapore Citizen or PR (at least one SC household member for PR cases)
- **Income guideline (PCHI):** ≤ **$800/month per capita** (raised from $650 in July 2023)
- **Alternative:** Household income ≤ ~$1,900/month
- **Also:** Little/no savings, inadequate family support
- **Duration:** 3–6 months per cycle, renewable
- **Amount:** Typical cash payout ~**$400–$800+/month** plus utility support (U-Save), often rental/S&CC waivers

### B. ComCare Long-Term Assistance (LTA) — "Public Assistance"

For people **permanently unable to work** due to old age, illness, or disability.

- **Who:** Singapore Citizen (PRs not eligible)
- **Criteria:** Permanently unable to work, no family support, minimal savings/assets
- **Duration:** Ongoing, reviewed periodically
- **2025/2026 amounts (from April 2025):**
  - **1-person household: $760/month** (up from $640)
  - 2-person and above: higher, scaled
- **Plus:** Free medical at polyclinics and public hospitals (MediFund), S&CC and utility subsidies, rental rebates

### C. ComCare Interim Assistance

For people in acute, immediate crisis — no food, electricity being cut, just lost job, facing eviction.

- **Duration: ≤3 months** (bridge while longer-term is sorted)
- **Form:** Cash, supermarket vouchers, food rations
- **Eligibility:** Same PCHI ≤$800 guideline, holistic assessment

### D. ComCare Student Care Fee Assistance (SCFA)

Subsidises fees at participating Student Care Centres. Same income framework.

## 2. BEFORE YOU GO — DOCUMENTS TO PREPARE

Put these in one folder. **If you don't have something, still go.**

**For every adult in the household:**
- NRIC (or FIN for PRs) — original + photocopy
- Birth certificates for children <15
- **Bank statements** — last 3 months, every account, every adult (most-requested doc)
- **CPF contribution history** — cpf.gov.sg → "My Statement" (12 months)
- **Payslips** — last 3 months, if employed
- **Employment contract** or termination letter if recently lost/changed jobs
- If self-employed or gig worker: written declaration plus any records

**For the household situation:**
- **Tenancy agreement** (if renting) or mortgage statement
- **Latest SP Services utility bill** and overdue notices
- **S&CC (Town Council) bill**
- **Debt letters** — credit cards, moneylender, fines, hospital bills

**If relevant:**
- **Medical certificates** (free at polyclinics)
- **Hospital discharge summaries**
- **School-fee bills** and children's report books
- **Insurance payout letters**
- **Disability documentation**

## 3. WHERE AND HOW TO APPLY

### Option 1: Walk into an SSO — recommended for first-timers

**24 SSOs** across Singapore. Go to the one **nearest your home**. **No appointment needed for first visit.**

**Typical hours:** Monday–Friday, 8:30am–6pm. Go in the morning; last walk-in ~5pm.

### Option 2: Apply online via SupportGoWhere

- Go to **supportgowhere.life.gov.sg**, click ComCare, login with **Singpass**
- Auto-fills via MyInfo; upload documents directly
- Works 24/7. SSO officer contacts you within a few working days
- Good if: you can use a computer, situation isn't same-day emergency, docs are digital

### Option 3: Apply on behalf of someone else

**Yes, you can.** Bring the person's NRIC + signed authorisation letter (plain paper). If they're bedridden, tell the officer — a social worker can arrange a home visit.

## 4. THE ASSESSMENT PROCESS

**Step 1 — Queue and registration (15–45 min).** Front-desk triages your case.

**Step 2 — Interview with Social Service Officer (45–90 min).** The officer will:
- Ask why you need help, in plain language. **Tell the whole story** — don't edit yourself
- Ask about every person in your household
- Ask about family outside the household — **the "family support test"**. Can your working son help? Why not? Estranged? Abusive? They need to hear this
- Pull your CPF, IRAS, HDB records via Singpass. **Do not hide anything**
- Look at savings, insurance, assets — **the "means test"**
- Decide: immediate help today, SMTA, LTA, or referral elsewhere

**Step 3 — Possible home visit (1–4 weeks).** Not every case gets one. LTA applications almost always include a home visit. **Not an inspection to trap you** — just confirming facts.

**Step 4 — Decision.** Typically **10–15 working days** after all documents are in. Letter or SMS.

## 5. WHAT YOU GET

### Cash assistance (main amount)

- **SMTA:** varies by household size. Typical ~$400–$800+/month
- **LTA:** **$760/month for one-person** (from April 2025), higher for larger households

### Add-ons (often included automatically)

- **Rental assistance / rent waiver** (HDB Public Rental Scheme)
- **S&CC rebates**
- **U-Save utility rebates**
- **Medical fee waiver / subsidy** via MediFund referral
- **School fee waiver and meal subsidies** (MOE FAS)
- **Student Care Centre fee subsidies (SCFA)**
- **Transport vouchers or bus concession**
- **Food rations / supermarket vouchers**
- **CDC Vouchers** — automatic $800 in FY2025 (no means test)

### Duration and renewal

- **SMTA:** 3–6 months/cycle, renewable
- **Interim:** ≤3 months
- **LTA:** ongoing, annual review
- **Start renewal ~1 month before cycle ends** — don't wait

## 6. IF YOU ARE REJECTED

### Common reasons

- Income or savings above threshold and no unusual hardship disclosed
- Household member who could support (e.g., working adult child) with no explanation
- Property ownership (more than place of residence)
- Incomplete documents
- Recent insurance payout or retrenchment benefit not declared

### Appeals process

1. **Ask for reasons in writing** — you are entitled
2. **Write an appeal letter** to the SSO Head or MSF. Include new facts or updated documents
3. **Request a case review** — another officer will re-examine
4. **Escalate via your MP's Meet-the-People Session (MPS)** — most-used informal appeal. Walk in, tell your story, MP writes to MSF. Free, very effective
5. **Call ComCare Hotline 1800-222-0000** and ask for escalation

### Alternative sources

- **Community Development Council (CDC)** in your district — emergency grants
- **Family Service Centres (50+)** — counselling plus short-term financial help
- **Religious organisations** — Catholic Welfare, Buddhist Federation, SIWEC, Muis (Zakat), churches, temples
- **Self-Help Groups** — CDAC, MENDAKI, SINDA, Eurasian Association
- **Ray of Hope, Food from the Heart, Willing Hearts** — food, one-off cash, meals
- **Credit Counselling Singapore (CCS)** — debt restructuring

## 7. ADJACENT SCHEMES — APPLY SIMULTANEOUSLY

Don't leave the SSO with only ComCare. Ask:

| Scheme | Who | Approx. benefit |
|--------|-----|-----------------|
| **Workfare Income Supplement (WIS)** | Low-wage workers 30+ (any age if disabled), income $500–$3,000 | Up to **$4,900–$7,200/year** cash + CPF |
| **GST Voucher – Cash** | Citizen, income ≤$39,000, home AV ≤$31,000 | **$450 or $850** (automatic August) |
| **GST Voucher – MediSave** | 65+ Citizen | Up to ~$450 |
| **GST Voucher – U-Save** | HDB households | Utility rebates |
| **Silver Support Scheme** | 65+ Citizen with low lifetime earnings | **$450–$1,080/quarter** (up to $4,320/year, automatic) |
| **MediFund** | Can't pay remaining public hospital bills | Applied through hospital MSW |
| **HDB Public Rental Scheme** | Household income ≤$1,500/month | **$26–$275/month rent** for 1–2-room flats |
| **MOE Financial Assistance Scheme** | Students, GHI ≤$4,000 or PCI ≤$1,000 | Full fee waiver, free textbooks/uniforms, meals, transport |
| **CHAS** | Household PCHI ≤ ~$2,300 (Blue) or ~$3,500 (Orange) | Subsidised GP and dental |
| **CDC Vouchers** | Every household — no means test | **$800 in 2025/2026** at go.gov.sg/cdcv |
| **Baby Bonus / KidSTART** | Families with young children | Cash gifts + CDA matching |

**The most important sentence to say at the SSO:** *"Please tell me every other scheme I should apply for given my situation."*

## 8. COMMON MISTAKES

1. **Waiting too long.** Go when your account drops below one month of expenses. ComCare is *supposed* to be used
2. **Assuming you'll be rejected because you "earn too much."** The $800 PCHI is a guideline. Bring evidence of *expenses* — that's what moves the needle
3. **Hiding income or savings.** Officers pull IRAS, CPF, bank data. Lies get caught and can lead to prosecution. Over-declare if in doubt
4. **Going to the wrong SSO.** Must be the one serving your residential address
5. **Going empty-handed and not coming back.** The case opens on visit 1 but assistance starts after documents. Bring the folder — or within 7 days
6. **Not explaining family support logic.** If officer says "your son can help" and you don't explain *why he can't*, the case goes cold
7. **Forgetting to mention medical issues.** Massively expands eligibility
8. **Giving up after one rejection.** Appeal. Visit MPS. 60–70% of escalated cases get revised outcomes
9. **Missing the renewal cycle** — mark the date
10. **Not using the free add-ons** — CHAS, MOE FAS, S&CC rebate. Ask for the whole bundle

## 9. TIMELINE: "I NEED HELP" → "PAYMENT RECEIVED"

| Day | What happens |
|-----|-------------|
| **Day 0** | Call **1800-222-0000** or walk into nearest SSO. Bring what you have |
| **Day 0 (same visit)** | Urgent interim — food vouchers, rations, sometimes same-day cash for severe cases |
| **Day 1–3** | Case file opened, officer pulls government records, requests missing docs |
| **Day 7–14** | Home visit (if needed), family-support checks, package drafted |
| **Day 10–15** | Formal decision by letter or SMS. Cash disbursed via PayNow/GIRO. **First payment usually within 2–3 weeks** |
| **Month 1+** | Monthly disbursement. Utility/S&CC rebates from next billing cycle |
| **Month 3–6** | Renewal review — prepare 1 month early |

## FINAL WORD

Singapore's safety net is genuinely designed to catch people. Officers deal with hundreds of families a week — yours is not a strange or shameful story. **Show up early, bring your documents, tell the truth, ask for everything you're entitled to.**
