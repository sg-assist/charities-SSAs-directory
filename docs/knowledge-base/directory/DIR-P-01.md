*If you're reading this in a hospital waiting room, breathe. The Singapore system is navigable. This guide walks you through it from the ER to long-term life at home.*

## STAGE 1 — THE FIRST 72 HOURS: ACUTE CARE

### Where your parent will be treated

Stroke patients brought in by SCDF ambulance are routed to the nearest hospital with an Acute Stroke Unit.

| Hospital | Phone | Notes |
|----------|-------|-------|
| **Tan Tock Seng Hospital (TTSH)** | 6256 6011 | NNI main campus (central) |
| **Singapore General Hospital (SGH)** | 6222 3322 | NNI main campus; door-to-thrombectomy ~80 min |
| **National University Hospital (NUH)** | 6779 5555 | Western Singapore |
| **National Neuroscience Institute (NNI)** | 6357 7153 | Operates across TTSH, SGH, CGH, KTPH, NTFGH |
| **Changi General Hospital (CGH)** | 6788 8833 | Eastern Singapore; houses NNI@CGH |
| **Khoo Teck Puat Hospital (KTPH)** | 6555 8000 | Northern Singapore |

### What happens in the stroke unit

- Door-to-needle clock starts immediately. For ischaemic strokes, clot-busting drugs (tPA) must be given within ~4.5 hours. Mechanical thrombectomy available at tertiary centres.
- A multidisciplinary team takes over: neurologist, stroke nurses, physiotherapist, OT, speech therapist, dietitian, and — crucially — a **Medical Social Worker (MSW)**.
- Scans: CT first, then MRI. Bloodwork, ECG, echocardiogram, carotid Doppler.
- Rehabilitation starts in the acute ward — often within 24–48 hours if stable.

### Typical length of acute admission

**Roughly 5–10 days.** SGH reports an average of ~6 days. Longer with complications.

### What you (the family) should do in Days 1–3

1. **Get the ward number and the name of the attending consultant.** Write it down.
2. **Ask to speak to the Medical Social Worker.** Do this on Day 2 at the latest. Do not wait for them to find you.
3. **Collect the patient's NRIC, CPF details, income documents, and insurance policies.** You will need these for subsidy and MediFund applications.
4. **Don't sign any discharge paperwork without asking where your parent is going next.**

### The family meeting / care conference

Most hospitals hold at least one case conference during the admission. **Attend it.** Questions to ask:

1. What is my parent's functional status now (ADLs: bathing, dressing, toileting, feeding, transferring, walking)?
2. What is the realistic prognosis in 3 months and 6 months?
3. Does the team recommend inpatient rehab (community hospital) or discharge home?
4. Will my parent need a feeding tube, urinary catheter, tracheostomy care?
5. What equipment will we need at home (hospital bed, wheelchair, commode, ripple mattress)?
6. What is the bill estimate and which subsidies apply?

### The four discharge destinations

1. **Home, independent** — rare after moderate/severe stroke
2. **Home with home care / day rehab** — for milder cases with family support
3. **Community hospital for inpatient rehab** — most common pathway after moderate stroke
4. **Nursing home** — for severe disability with no family able to provide 24/7 care

### Agency for Integrated Care (AIC) — the coordinator

- **AIC Hotline: 1800-650-6060**
- Website: aic.sg
- Walk in: Any AIC Link (at major polyclinics and malls)

## STAGE 3 — THE COMMUNITY HOSPITAL PATHWAY

### When a community hospital (CH) is recommended

The acute hospital refers to a CH when your parent is medically stable but still needs daily intensive therapy (2–3 hours/day). Standard for moderate-to-severe strokes with hemiparesis, aphasia, or dysphagia.

### Typical stay

**~3–4 weeks (21–30 days).** Median in Singapore data: 29 days. Some stay up to 6 weeks.

### Subsidies

- Means-tested by MOH. Citizens up to **75% subsidy**; PRs ~half that.
- **MediSave:** up to **$250/day** of the bill
- **MediShield Life** covers part of daily ward charges
- **MediFund** is the safety net — apply via the CH's MSW

## STAGE 4 — REHABILITATION

Stroke recovery is a 6–12 month window where intensity matters.

### Inpatient vs day rehab vs home therapy

- **Inpatient (CH):** 2–3 hours therapy/day, 5–6 days/week. Best for early, severe cases
- **Day Rehabilitation Centre (DRC):** 2–5 times/week, transport provided. Best once they can transfer and travel
- **Home-based therapy:** a therapist visits the flat. For patients too frail to travel

### The three therapies

- **Physiotherapy (PT):** gait, balance, strength, transfers
- **Occupational therapy (OT):** ADLs, upper-limb function, home assessment, equipment
- **Speech and language therapy (SLT):** aphasia, dysarthria, **dysphagia** (critical to prevent aspiration pneumonia)

## STAGE 5 — HOME MODIFICATIONS

**Start the application while your parent is still in the CH**, so the flat is ready on discharge day.

### HDB EASE (Enhancement for Active Seniors)

Subsidises grab bars, slip-resistant floor treatment, and ramps in HDB flats.

- **Eligibility:** household member 65+, or 60–64 needing help with ≥1 ADL (stroke patients usually qualify)
- **Subsidy:** up to **95%** for 1–3-room flats
- **Covers:** up to 10 grab bars, slip-resistant treatment in 2 bathrooms, up to 5 ramps
- **Apply:** HDB Branch e-Service or Mobile@HDB app. Turnaround ~1 month
- **HDB hotline: 1800-225-5432**

### Seniors' Mobility and Enabling Fund (SMF)

Pays for wheelchairs, walking aids, hospital beds, ripple mattresses, motorised wheelchairs, hearing aids.

- **Eligibility:** SG citizen, 60+, household income ≤$2,000/person (or AV ≤$13,000)
- **Subsidy:** up to **90%** of cost
- **Apply:** via CH's OT or MSW, or AIC online at aic.sg or **1800-650-6060**

### SG Enable — for wheelchair users under 60

- Tel: **1800-858-5885**
- Website: sgenable.sg
- Runs the Assistive Technology Fund (ATF)

## STAGE 6 — FINANCIAL ASSISTANCE

### 1. MediFund — the hospital safety net

For citizens who cannot pay remaining bills after subsidies, MediShield Life, and MediSave. Apply **through the hospital MSW**.

### 2. Home Caregiving Grant (HCG)

- **$400/month** (increasing to **$600/month** from April 2026)
- **Eligibility:** care recipient needs help with ≥3 of 6 ADLs; PCHI ≤$3,600; not in a nursing home
- **Apply:** via AIC. Need a **Functional Assessment Report (FAR)** — the CH's doctor/therapist can do this before discharge
- **AIC:** 1800-650-6060. Processing 6–8 weeks

### 3. CareShield Life / ElderShield

- **Trigger:** unable to perform ≥3 of 6 ADLs
- **CareShield Life 2026:** ~$689/month, increasing 4% annually
- **ElderShield:** fixed $300 or $400/month for up to 60–72 months
- **To claim:** contact an **MOH-accredited severe disability assessor** (list at careshieldlife.gov.sg). Assessment fee reimbursed if claim succeeds
- **Hotline: 1800-227-1188**

### 4. FDW Levy Concession

- Standard levy: $300/month → concession **$60/month**
- **Eligibility:** care recipient certified to need help with ≥1 ADL
- Automatic for those caring for 67+; otherwise apply via AIC

## STAGE 8 — CAREGIVER SUPPORT

### Caregivers Training Grant (CTG)

- **$400 first year, $200 annual top-up**
- Pays for caregiver training (transfers, feeding, wound care, dementia care)
- Apply via AIC: **1800-650-6060**

### Respite care

- **Nursing Home Respite Care:** short stays, up to 1 month. Daily rate from ~$131 before subsidies
- **Centre-based respite:** NTUC Health, Presbyterian Community Services, AWWA
- **Home-based respite:** trained care worker visits the flat
- All arranged through AIC

### Caregiver organisations

- **Caregivers Alliance Limited (CAL):** 6460 4400; cal.org.sg
- **Homage** (private): 6100 0055
- **Jaga-Me** (private): 6717 3737

## STAGE 9 — THE BIG DECISIONS

### Decision 1: Home care vs nursing home

**Home care factors:** family available, flat can be modified, patient motivated, cognition preserved, informal support

**Nursing home factors:** severe disability with 24/7 needs, no home support, multiple comorbidities, caregiver elderly/unwell

### Decision 2: Do I (or a sibling) stop work?

- Check employer's unpaid family care leave and flexi-work options
- **Run the math:** FDW at $700 + $60 levy + food is often cheaper than lost salary. HCG offsets half
- Stroke caregiving averages 3–7 years; some last a decade
- Talk to CAL or S3 before deciding

### Decision 3: Hiring a Foreign Domestic Worker (FDW)

- Start while parent is still in the CH (2–3 weeks for MOM approval + arrival)
- Use an MOM-accredited Employment Agency
- MOM requires caregiver training modules
- Apply levy concession simultaneously
- Buy good medical + personal accident insurance

### Decision 4: LPA and Advance Care Planning

If your parent still has mental capacity, do this now:
- **LPA:** Office of the Public Guardian, 6354 8100; publicguardian.gov.sg. Fees waived for citizens
- **ACP:** the hospital has ACP facilitators — ask the MSW. Free

## A ROUGH TIMELINE

| Week | What's happening | What you do |
|------|------------------|-------------|
| **1** | Acute hospital | Find the MSW. Bring NRIC + income docs. Attend case conference |
| **1–2** | Transfer to CH | Ask about CH choice. Start EASE application. Ask about HCG Functional Assessment |
| **2–5** | Inpatient rehab | Attend therapy weekly to learn exercises. Complete caregiver training. Arrange FDW. Arrange equipment via SMF |
| **4–6** | Discharge | Install grab bars/ramps before discharge. Have equipment delivered. Confirm day rehab referral |
| **Month 2–6** | Outpatient rehab | Start day rehab. Join S3 or SNSA. Watch for depression |
| **Month 6–12** | Long-term adjustment | Reassess HCG, SMF, review LPA. Caregiver takes respite at least once |
