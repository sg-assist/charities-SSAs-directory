## 1. WHEN TO CONSIDER A NURSING HOME

Consider it when any of the following apply:

- **24-hour nursing needs**: tube feeding, tracheostomy care, pressure-injury management, IV meds, frequent suctioning, oxygen dependency
- **Severe physical disability**: bed-bound or wheelchair-bound with full dependence in ADLs (MOH Category 3 or 4 care)
- **Advanced dementia with behavioural symptoms**: wandering, aggression, sundowning, swallowing impairment home caregivers cannot manage
- **Caregiver burnout / collapse**: primary caregiver ill, exhausted, had to quit work; respite no longer enough
- **No home support**: senior lives alone, family overseas, unsuitable flat
- **Repeated hospital admissions**: recurrent falls, aspiration pneumonia, UTIs, pressure sores

**How you know it's time:** senior's safety is at risk, caregiver's own health is breaking, or the hospital discharge planner raises it. **Do not wait for a crisis** — subsidised placement takes weeks.

## 2. ASSESSMENT BY A MEDICAL SOCIAL WORKER (MSW)

**You cannot self-apply directly to AIC for subsidised care** — you need an MSW or doctor's referral.

### Where to find an MSW

- **If hospitalised:** ask the ward nurse to page Medical Social Work. Every public hospital has one
- **If at polyclinic:** ask the doctor for a referral
- **If at home:** ask AIC (1800-650-6060) to refer to a community MSW or arrange a house call

### What the MSW does

1. Takes a social history — family structure, finances, caregiving, housing
2. Coordinates a **medical assessment** — Resident Assessment Form (RAF) by a doctor detailing diagnoses, ADLs, cognition, continence, nursing needs
3. Performs **financial assessment** (means test) and gathers income documents
4. Submits to AIC via the **Integrated Referral Management System (IRMS)**

### Documents to prepare

**For the patient:**
- NRIC
- Latest medical report / discharge summary
- List of medications
- CPF LIFE / MediSave statements
- CareShield Life / ElderShield certificate
- Pioneer / Merdeka Generation card (if applicable)

**For the household (means test):**
- NRICs of all household members
- 3 months' payslips OR latest IRAS Notice of Assessment
- CPF contribution history
- HDB tenancy or property title
- Property Annual Value from IRAS mytax portal (for zero-income cases)

## 3. AIC'S ROLE — THE CENTRAL COORDINATOR

The **Agency for Integrated Care (AIC)** is Singapore's single clearinghouse for MOH-subsidised long-term care.

- **AIC Hotline: 1800-650-6060** (Mon–Fri 8:30am–8:30pm, Sat 8:30am–4pm)
- Email: enquiries@aic.sg
- **AIC Link walk-in centres** at Jurong Point, Toa Payoh HDB Hub, Tampines, etc. Bring NRIC

### What AIC does

1. **Verifies eligibility** and applies the means test
2. **Matches the senior to available beds** across the ~88 nursing homes, based on care level, location, language/religion, urgency
3. **Maintains the waitlist** and notifies the family when a bed is offered
4. **Arranges bridging services** while waiting — home nursing, day care, or Interim Caregiver Scheme

Families can list **up to 3 nursing home preferences**; AIC tries to honour them but offers the first suitable vacancy.

## 4. CHOOSING A NURSING HOME

Singapore has ~**88 nursing homes**: ~**48 VWO-run** (subsidised), ~**40 private**.

### VWO vs Private — the trade-off

| Factor | VWO | Private |
|--------|-----|---------|
| Subsidised beds | Yes, main route | Some (Portable Subsidies at approved homes) |
| Waiting list | Longer (weeks–months) | Shorter; immediate self-paying |
| Cost before subsidy | ~$1,300–$3,500/month | ~$1,200–$4,500+ (luxury $10,000+) |
| Ambience | Institutional; dormitory-style | Hotel-like, more single/double rooms |
| Staff ratios | MOH-regulated minimums | Often higher in premium |
| Dementia-specific | Apex Harmony Lodge, others | Orange Valley, Allium |

### Factors to weigh

- **Location** — can family visit weekly?
- **Religion & dietary needs** — Jamiyah (halal), Sree Narayana Mission (vegetarian), Kwong Wai Shiu (Chinese), St Andrew's (Christian), MWS (Methodist)
- **Dementia capability** — secured ward? trained staff? structured activities?
- **Language / dialect** — Mandarin common; Hokkien, Teochew, Cantonese, Malay, Tamil varies
- **Visiting policies** — most homes allow daily visits; some restrict to weekends
- **Ward type** — 8–10-bed cubicles (subsidised), 4-bed, 2-bed, or single

**Tour checklist:** smell the wards (ammonia = poor toileting), watch staff-resident interactions, check if residents are groomed and out of bed, look at meal presentation, ask to see the physio room.

## 5. SUBSIDY ASSESSMENT (MEANS TEST)

Based on **Monthly Per Capita Household Income (PCHI)** = total gross household income ÷ number of members. If PCHI is zero, the **Annual Value (AV) of residence** is used instead.

### Residential LTC subsidy tiers (enhanced from July 2026, Singapore Citizens)

| Monthly PCHI | AV (if zero income) | Max Subsidy |
|-------------|---------------------|-------------|
| ≤$900 | ≤$13,000 | **80%** |
| $901–$1,500 | — | 60% |
| $1,501–$2,300 | $13,001–$21,000 | 50% |
| $2,301–$2,600 | — | 40% |
| $2,601–$3,600 | $21,001–$25,000 | 20% |
| $3,601–$4,800 | — | 15% |
| ≥$4,801 | >$25,000 | 0% |

- **PRs** get roughly half the SC subsidy at each tier
- **Pioneer Generation** and **Merdeka Generation** get additional top-ups
- A transitional fee rebate runs July 2025–June 2026 before the enhanced tiers

**Example:** A low-income SG citizen in a VWO home costing $2,500/month may end up paying as little as $500 out of pocket after the 80% subsidy, before CareShield Life payouts and MediFund.

### MediFund

Applies **after** subsidies — safety net for residents who cannot afford the balance. The home's MSW files the application.

## 6. WAITING TIMES

- **Median wait for subsidised bed:** ~**1 month** for general care
- **Urgent medical cases:** prioritised — hours to days (MOH reports avg urgent placement ~7 hours)
- **Preferred-home wait:** 3–12 months for a specific VWO home, depending on ward type and gender
- **Private homes (self-pay):** immediate — usually within days

### Bridging options while waiting

- **Interim Caregiver Scheme (ICS):** post-discharge, 6 days/week, up to 12 hours/day home custodial care
- **Respite care** in a nursing home: short stays 1–30 days at subsidised rates
- **Home nursing / home personal care** via AIC-approved providers
- **Community Hospital** step-down if rehab is still beneficial
- **Private self-pay nursing home temporarily**, then transfer

## 7. MONTHLY COSTS

| Home Type | Range (per month, pre-subsidy) |
|-----------|-------------------------------|
| VWO nursing homes | **$1,300–$3,500** |
| Private (standard) | **$1,200–$4,500** |
| Private (premium) | **$4,960–$5,890** |
| Luxury (Allium Care Suites) | **$9,000–$15,000+** |

**Specific examples:**
- **Ren Ci:** ~$700–$1,600 after subsidy for Cat 3/4
- **St Andrew's:** $1,300–$2,300
- **NTUC Health:** $2,000–$3,500 standard; up to $5,890 newer
- **Kwong Wai Shiu:** $1,460–$2,407
- **Apex Harmony Lodge** (dementia): $3,150–$3,400

**After 75–80% subsidy, a typical low-income Singaporean family pays ~$300–$700/month** out of pocket, reduced further by CareShield Life ($600–$1,200/month) and MediFund.

**What the monthly fee covers:** accommodation, nursing, meals, basic meds (formulary), routine doctor rounds, physiotherapy, incontinence supplies, laundry.

**Extras billed separately:** specialist consults, hospital transfers, diapers beyond allotment, non-formulary drugs, dental, podiatry, personal items.

## 8. THE ADMISSION PROCESS — STEP BY STEP

1. **Pre-admission visit** — Tour 2–3 shortlisted homes. See the actual ward. Meet the nurse manager
2. **Interview / intake assessment** — Home's nurse and MSW interview family and review documents
3. **Bed offer from AIC** — Usually by phone/SMS. **Limited window (1–3 days) to accept**
4. **Trial stay / respite** (optional) — 2-week respite placement to assess fit. Helpful for dementia
5. **Admission day checklist:**
   - 7–10 sets of loose, labelled clothing
   - Non-slip shoes/slippers
   - Toiletries: toothbrush, soap, shampoo, shaver, denture cup
   - Towels and face cloths
   - Spectacles, hearing aids (spare batteries), dentures (labelled case)
   - Walking aids, wheelchair cushion
   - A familiar item — photo frame, rosary, favourite blanket
   - Full medication supply (original bottles)
   - All documents
   - **Do NOT bring:** cash, jewellery, credit cards, valuables
6. **First-week adjustment** — Expect distress. Visit daily if possible. Most settle within 2–4 weeks

## 9. ONGOING CARE

- **Family involvement:** attend monthly/quarterly **care conferences** (doctor, nurse, physio, MSW) — review care goals, medication, mobility, end-of-life preferences
- **Advance Care Planning (ACP):** most homes offer this. Cover resuscitation, hospital transfers, preferred place of death. Do it early
- **Reporting concerns:**
  1. Raise first with shift nurse
  2. Escalate to Nurse Manager, then Administrator
  3. Written feedback to Patient Relations Officer
  4. If unresolved: **MOH Feedback** (moh.gov.sg/feedback or **6325 9220**). Homes must notify MOH of abuse within 72 hours
- **Transferring homes:** go back through AIC. You keep subsidy status if within a year

## FINAL PRACTICAL TIPS

1. **Start early** — begin MSW referral the moment hospitalisation is expected to end in placement. Do not wait for discharge day
2. **Get an LPA** — if senior still has capacity, do a Lasting Power of Attorney *now*. Without one, deputyship via Family Court takes months
3. **List realistic preferences** — insisting on one home can cost 6+ months of waiting. List 3
4. **Visit before you sign** — never admit a loved one to a home you haven't toured personally
5. **Keep receipts** — for MediFund applications and tax relief (parent/handicapped parent relief)
6. **Don't feel guilty** — a nursing home placement is often the most loving and safest choice when home care has reached its limit
