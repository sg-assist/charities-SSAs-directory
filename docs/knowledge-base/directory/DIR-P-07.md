*If you are reading this because something is happening at home that scares you — you are not alone and it is not your fault. Singapore has real resources, real shelters, and real legal protection. This guide tells you where to start.*

---

## THE MOST IMPORTANT THING TO KNOW

**If you are in immediate danger, call 999 right now.** Police in Singapore take family violence seriously. You do not need to have visible injuries to call. You do not need to decide to press charges before you call.

If you cannot speak on the phone, call 999 and stay on the line. The operator is trained to help.

---

## 1. IMMEDIATE SAFETY — IF YOU ARE IN DANGER NOW

### Call 999

Police will attend. They can arrest an abuser who is committing or has just committed a criminal offence. They can remove the abuser from the home if there is imminent danger.

When police attend a family violence incident, they are required to make a referral to social services — this is standard procedure, not a punishment.

### Crisis shelters — safe housing tonight

If you need to leave home immediately, shelters can take you in without prior booking (subject to availability). Call before you leave if possible.

| Shelter | Who it serves | Number |
|---------|---------------|--------|
| **PAVE Integrated Services** | Women and children fleeing family violence | **6555 0390** (helpline) |
| **TRANS SAFE Centre** | Women and children; also serves Malay/Muslim families | **6799 8858** |
| **SHINE Children and Youth Services (Fei Yue)** | Women and children | **6476 1482** |
| **Calvary Community Care** | Women and children | **6354 4220** |
| **AWARE SafePlace** | Women fleeing intimate partner violence | **1800 777 5555** (AWARE helpline) |

**What to bring if you can (do not delay leaving if you cannot):**
- NRIC / passports for yourself and children
- Medication
- Some cash or access to bank account
- Children's essential items (milk powder, diapers)
- School materials for children
- Any documents proving the violence (medical reports, photos, police reports)

Shelters are **confidential.** The abuser is not told where you are.

### Leaving safely: a quick checklist

- [ ] Know the emergency number: **999**
- [ ] Have a packed bag ready if possible, stored with a trusted person or at work
- [ ] Tell at least one trusted person your situation
- [ ] Note the shelter numbers in your phone under a neutral name
- [ ] If you use shared devices, clear your browser history after reading this

## 2. CRISIS SHELTERS AND SAFE HOUSING

Shelters are more than a place to sleep. They provide:
- Safe, confidential accommodation for you and your children
- Counselling and case management
- Help applying for a Personal Protection Order
- Assistance accessing financial and legal help
- Help with children's school transfers if needed

### Length of stay

Shelter stays typically range from a few weeks to several months, depending on your circumstances and the time needed to make a longer-term plan.

### For those who cannot use a shelter

If you need to remain at home (for example, if the abuser has left or you do not have children and do not feel in immediate danger), you can still access all the support services listed in this guide. You do not need to have left home to apply for a PPO or speak to a social worker.

## 3. PERSONAL PROTECTION ORDER (PPO) — LEGAL PROTECTION

A Personal Protection Order (PPO) is a court order prohibiting the respondent (the person who has committed violence) from using family violence against you. Breaching a PPO is a criminal offence punishable by fine and/or imprisonment.

### Who can apply

Any family member who has experienced family violence or is at risk. "Family member" under the Women's Charter includes:
- Spouse or former spouse
- Child (including adopted or step-child)
- Parent or step-parent
- Sibling
- Any person who is a member of the same household

### How to apply

**Step 1 — File at the Family Justice Courts (FJC)**

- **Where:** Family Justice Courts, 3 Havelock Square, S(059725)
- **Helpline: 6435 5077**
- **Website:** familyjusticecourts.gov.sg
- **Hours:** Monday–Friday, 8:30am–5:30pm
- You can file personally or be assisted by a social worker or lawyer

**Step 2 — Submit the application form**

The court's Service Bureau will assist you with Form 275 (Application for PPO). You will need to describe the incidents of violence. You do not need a police report to apply, but having one helps.

**Step 3 — Hearing**

A judge will review your application. In serious cases, an **Expedited Order (EO)** can be granted on the same day — this takes effect immediately while the main PPO application proceeds. For cases with children, a **Mandatory Care Order (MCO)** can also be made, requiring the abuser to attend counselling.

**Step 4 — If the PPO is breached**

Call 999 immediately and show the officer the PPO. Breach is a criminal offence. Keep a copy of your PPO on your phone and in physical form.

### What the PPO does NOT do

A PPO is a civil order, not a criminal conviction. It does not automatically resolve divorce, custody, or financial matters — those require separate proceedings.

## 4. THE ROLE OF MSF AND FAMILY SERVICE CENTRES

### Family Service Centres (FSCs)

Your local FSC should be one of your first calls after immediate safety is secured. FSCs provide free, confidential counselling and can coordinate your access to multiple services.

- **50+ FSCs** across Singapore. Find yours at: go.gov.sg/fsc or call **1800-222-0000**
- No referral needed. Walk in or call.
- Services include: individual and family counselling, crisis intervention, safety planning, referrals to shelter, PPO assistance, financial help

### MSF Child Protective Services

If there are children in the household, MSF may be involved for child protection purposes. This is not meant to take children away from the non-abusive parent — it is designed to ensure children are safe.

If a child is at risk, you or any professional can make a report to the **Child Protective Service (CPS):**
- **MSF CPS Hotline: 1800-777-0000** (24 hours)

A social worker will assess the situation. The aim is to support the family and keep children safe, usually with the non-abusive parent.

## 5. LEGAL AID AND ADVICE

### Legal Aid Bureau (LAB)

Provides **free or subsidised legal representation** for civil matters including divorce, PPO applications, and custody — for those who meet the means and merits test.

- **Hotline: 1800-225-1424**
- **Website:** lab.mlaw.gov.sg
- **Address:** 45 Maxwell Road, The URA Centre, S(069118)
- **Qualifying income (indicative):** per capita monthly income ≤~$950 (precise threshold set by LAB)

### AWARE Legal Clinic

**AWARE (Association of Women for Action and Research)** runs a free legal clinic for women on family law matters including divorce, custody, PPOs, and property division.

- **AWARE Helpline: 1800 777 5555** (Monday–Friday, 10am–6pm)
- **Website:** aware.org.sg
- Clinic appointments are bookable via the helpline
- Services are for women; men can call the helpline for referrals

### Syariah Court (for Muslim families)

For Muslim families, divorce and related matters are governed by the Administration of Muslim Law Act and heard at the Syariah Court.

- **Syariah Court: 6359 1249**
- **Website:** syariahcourt.gov.sg
- PPO applications are still made at the Family Justice Courts regardless of religion

## 6. SUPPORT FOR CHILDREN

Children in households with family violence are affected even if they are not directly targeted. They may experience trauma symptoms (nightmares, regression, aggression, withdrawal) and need their own support.

### What to tell children

Age-appropriate honesty is best: *"What happened in our home was wrong. It was not your fault. We are going to be safe now."*

### MSF Child Protective Services

If you are concerned a child has been directly abused (physically or sexually), contact CPS at **1800-777-0000**.

### School support

When children change schools due to family violence relocation, inform the new school counsellor (every school has one). Schools can flag welfare concerns and provide a stable environment during upheaval.

### Specialist support for children

| Service | Notes | Contact |
|---------|-------|---------|
| **PAVE** | Child-focused counselling alongside family violence services | 6555 0390 |
| **SHINE (Fei Yue)** | Child counselling, play therapy | 6476 1482 |
| **AWARE SASH Programme** | Support for sexual assault survivors including children | 6779 7137 |
| **School Counsellors** | In every MOE school; free | Via school |

## 7. FINANCIAL SUPPORT DURING SEPARATION

Leaving an abusive situation often means losing access to shared income or finances. Help is available.

### ComCare Immediate Financial Assistance

For urgent financial need — food, bills, rent. Apply at any **Social Service Office (SSO)**.

- **ComCare Hotline: 1800-222-0000**
- Emergency assistance can be granted on the same day in acute cases
- You do not need to have been through a divorce to apply

### SCWO (Singapore Council of Women's Organisations)

SCWO's CRISP programme provides short-term financial assistance specifically for women in crisis.

- **Tel: 6571 0200**
- **Website:** scwo.org.sg

### Emergency food and essential supplies

Shelters typically provide meals. If you are not in a shelter:
- **Food from the Heart: 6280 4483**
- **The Food Bank Singapore: 6542 3361**
- **Willing Hearts (daily meals): 6441 6468**

### Interim maintenance

If you are separated from a spouse, you can apply for **interim maintenance** through the Family Justice Courts while divorce proceedings are underway. A social worker at an FSC or a legal aid lawyer can help you with this application.

## 8. LONGER-TERM RECOVERY — COUNSELLING AND REHABILITATION

Recovery from family violence is a process, not an event. The following services provide ongoing support:

### PAVE Integrated Services

Specialist family violence service offering individual and group counselling, legal support, shelter, and community support.

- **Helpline: 6555 0390**
- **Website:** pave.org.sg
- Serves women, men, elderly, and children

### AWARE SASH (Support, Assistance and Specialist Housing) Programme

For women who have experienced sexual assault, intimate partner violence, or family violence.

- **Sexual assault helpline: 6779 7137** (Monday–Friday, 10am–6pm)
- **Website:** aware.org.sg/sash

### FSC Counselling

Your local FSC provides ongoing counselling at no cost for you and your children. They can also help with practical matters (housing, finance, children) as part of the same case management relationship.

### Specialist counselling for trauma

- **Trauma-focused CBT and EMDR** are available at IMH outpatient services and some private practitioners
- **IMH outpatient: 6389 2000** (requires GP or self-referral)
- Some VWOs and FSCs have trained trauma counsellors

## 9. FOR MEN EXPERIENCING FAMILY VIOLENCE

Family violence affects men too, including from intimate partners, parents, or siblings. Men may face additional barriers to help-seeking.

### TACT (Triage, Assessment and Crisis support for abused men)

**MSF-funded programme**, operated as part of the family violence response ecosystem. Provides counselling, safety planning, and referrals for men who experience family violence.

- Contact via: **PAVE Helpline 6555 0390** (PAVE operates TACT services)
- Also accessible via any FSC

### Police and PPO

Men can call 999 and apply for PPOs in exactly the same way as any other victim of family violence. The legal protections are identical.

### AWARE Helpline

The AWARE helpline (1800 777 5555) can also advise men on what services are available and make referrals.

## FIRST THREE STEPS IF YOU ARE UNSAFE NOW

1. **If in immediate danger:** Call **999**
2. **If you need to leave tonight:** Call **PAVE 6555 0390** or **TRANS SAFE 6799 8858** — they can arrange shelter
3. **For everything else — counselling, PPO, finances:** Walk into your nearest FSC (find it at go.gov.sg/fsc) or call **ComCare 1800-222-0000**

## A NOTE FOR SOCIAL WORKERS AND HELPERS

If a client discloses family violence to you:

- Acknowledge, believe, and affirm: *"I'm glad you told me. This is not your fault."*
- Conduct a **safety assessment** — is there imminent danger? Are children at risk?
- Contact CPS if there are children at risk: **1800-777-0000**
- Offer the client choices. Safety planning respects their autonomy.
- Do not pressure a client to leave — leaving is the most dangerous time in many cases. Safety planning *before* leaving saves lives.
- PAVE and AWARE provide professional consultation for workers handling family violence cases.

You are not alone in this work either.
