## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Singapore's children and youth services span prevention, early intervention, protection, residential care, and reintegration. Services are coordinated across the Ministry of Social and Family Development (MSF), Ministry of Education (MOE), and a wide network of voluntary welfare organisations (VWOs) and social service agencies (SSAs).

Key entry points include Family Service Centres (FSCs) for family support and counselling, Social Service Offices (SSOs) for financial assistance, and MSF Child Protective Services for cases involving abuse or neglect. Early intervention for children with developmental needs is accessed through EIPIC providers under SG Enable. Residential care, after-school student care, and youth-at-risk programmes are delivered by a broad range of SSAs, including racially constituted self-help groups (CDAC, Mendaki, SINDA, Eurasian Association) and faith-based and secular VWOs.

This guide covers government programmes, voluntary homes, student care, youth-at-risk services, counselling, adoption and foster care, and key hotlines.

## KEY GOVERNMENT PROGRAMMES

### KidSTART
A preventive home visiting and group-based programme for children aged 0–6 from lower-income families, designed to support early childhood development and school readiness. Operated by NCSS and partner SSAs, KidSTART connects families with community resources and provides structured learning activities.

- **Eligibility:** Children aged 0–6 in households with per capita income ≤$1,400 or receiving ComCare assistance
- **Services:** Home visits, learning and developmental activities, parent coaching, health referrals
- **Referral:** Via Family Service Centres or Social Service Offices
- **Website:** kidstart.sg

### UPLIFT (Uplifting Pupils in Life and Inspiring Families Taskforce)
An inter-agency initiative targeting students from disadvantaged backgrounds who need additional support in school attendance, learning, and overall wellbeing. UPLIFT coordinates between schools, FSCs, and community partners to provide wrap-around support.

- **Access:** Via school counsellors, Form Teachers, or FSC referral

### Student Care Fee Assistance (SCFA)
MSF financial assistance scheme to subsidise the fees of approved student care centres for children from lower-income families.

- **Eligibility:** Gross household income ≤$4,500/month or per capita income ≤$1,125; child attending an MOE-registered student care centre
- **Subsidy:** Up to 98% of student care fees depending on income tier
- **Apply at:** Student care centre operator or Social Service Office

### MOE Financial Assistance Scheme (FAS)
MOE's scheme providing bursaries, free textbooks, uniforms, and school meal subsidies for students in government and government-aided schools.

- **Eligibility:** Gross household income ≤$3,000/month or per capita income ≤$750
- **Benefits:** Full school fees waiver, free textbooks and uniforms, CCA subsidies, school meal subsidies
- **Apply at:** Child's school

### MSF Child Protection Services
MSF investigates reports of child abuse, neglect, and exploitation. Child Protection Officers assess risk, coordinate interventions, and may apply for court orders where necessary. Cases may be referred to Family Service Centres or specialist Child Protection Specialist Centres.

- **Report abuse or concern:** Call **1800-777-0000** (24 hours)
- **Child Protection Specialist Centres:** Big Love (6445 0400), TRANS SAFE Centre (6909 0315)

### EIPIC (Early Intervention Programme for Infants and Children)
EIPIC provides developmental therapy and support for children aged 0–6 with disabilities or developmental delays. Programmes are delivered by SG Enable-appointed EIPIC centres and cover speech therapy, occupational therapy, physiotherapy, and developmental education.

- **Eligibility:** Children aged 0–6 with diagnosed developmental delays or disabilities
- **Funding:** MOE Kindergarten Financial Assistance Scheme (KiFAS) and MSF subsidies applicable
- **Referral:** Via paediatrician, polyclinic, or SG Enable (1800-858-8585)
- **Pathway note:** For a full early intervention pathway for children with special needs, see **DIR-P-05 (Child with Special Needs)**

## YOUTH-AT-RISK SERVICES

### Singapore Boys' Home
MSF-gazetted approved school for boys aged 7–16 ordered by the court. Provides education, rehabilitation, and reintegration support. Boys' Town also operates youth outreach programmes for boys not under court order.

- **Operator:** Ministry of Social and Family Development
- **Phone (Boys' Town):** 6690 5420 | **Website:** boystown.org.sg

### Singapore Girls' Home
MSF-gazetted approved school for girls aged 7–18 ordered by the court. Provides education, therapy, counselling, and family reintegration support.

- **Operator:** Ministry of Social and Family Development
- **Phone:** 6775 3041

### Fei Yue Community Services
Provides counselling, casework, youth outreach, and residential support including a youth shelter. Operates multiple FSCs and community development programmes.

- **Phone:** 6762 5215 (main) | **Hotline (TOUCHline):** 1800-377-2252
- **Website:** fycs.org

### Limitless (formerly Boys' Brigade Advocacy Centre)
Youth-at-risk intervention providing street outreach, mentoring, and empowerment programmes for youths aged 7–35 in vulnerable situations including gang involvement, substance misuse, and school disengagement.

- **Phone:** 6339 9170
- **Website:** limitless.sg

## COUNSELLING AND MENTAL HEALTH SUPPORT

### Samaritans of Singapore (SOS)
24-hour crisis support for individuals in emotional distress or at risk of suicide. Available to all ages including children and youth.

- **Hotline:** 1800-221-4444 (24 hours, toll-free)
- **CareText:** SMS/chat via careline@sos.org.sg
- **Website:** sos.org.sg

### TOUCHline
Helpline operated by TOUCH Community Services offering emotional support and referrals for children, youth, and families.

- **Hotline:** 1800-377-2252
- **Website:** touch.org.sg

### Care Corner Counselling Centre
Provides affordable individual, family, and group counselling across multiple locations in Singapore, including targeted services for youths and families under stress.

- **Phone:** 6353 1180
- **Website:** carecorner.org.sg

### School Counsellors and School-Based Family Violence Pilot Programmes
Every MOE school has a trained counsellor. For students requiring more intensive support, referrals can be made to FSCs, SSAs, or Child Guidance Clinics at IMH or KKH.

- **Child Guidance Clinic (KK Women's and Children's Hospital):** 6225 5554
- **Child Guidance Clinic (IMH @ Bishan):** 6389 2000

## ADOPTION AND FOSTER CARE

### MSF Adoption Services
MSF oversees the regulatory framework for local and inter-country adoption in Singapore. Adoptive parents must meet eligibility criteria and complete a home study assessment.

- **Local adoption:** Applications submitted to MSF; inter-country adoption requires a Home Study Report
- **Phone:** 1800-222-0000
- **Website:** msf.gov.sg (search "adoption")

### HEART Adoption Centre (Home, Equity, Respect and Transformation)
Singapore's primary voluntary adoption service provider, facilitating adoptions and providing post-adoption support services.

- **Phone:** 6274 0308
- **Email:** info@heartadoption.org.sg
- **Website:** heartadoption.org.sg

### Foster Care (MSF Foster Care Service)
MSF coordinates the placement of children in need with trained foster families. Foster parents receive training, a monthly allowance, and professional support.

- **Enquiries:** 1800-222-0000 or email MSF
- **Website:** msf.gov.sg (search "foster care")

## PATHWAY NOTE — CHILDREN WITH SPECIAL NEEDS

For children with developmental disabilities, autism, Down syndrome, or physical disabilities, the primary entry points for early intervention, therapy, and education are different from mainstream children's services. See **DIR-P-05 (Child with Special Needs)** for the full early intervention pathway, SPED school options, SG Enable services, and disability-specific SSAs.
