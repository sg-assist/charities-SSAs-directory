## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Singapore's employment support landscape spans government-led job matching agencies, Skills-based training schemes, supported employment for persons with disabilities, reintegration programmes for ex-offenders, employment assistance for seniors, and financial bridges for those between jobs.

The principal government bodies are Workforce Singapore (WSG), which oversees national employment strategy and retraining, and the Employment and Employability Institute (e2i), operated by NTUC, which provides direct job placement and career coaching. SkillsFuture Singapore (SSG) administers SkillsFuture Credit and national skills upgrading. For populations facing structural barriers to employment — persons with disabilities, ex-offenders, and older workers — specialised programmes and social service agencies provide supported employment, job carving, and employer engagement.

Social workers and caregivers should note that financial assistance (ComCare, CDC Vouchers) can bridge income gaps during job search, and that referral pathways to WSG Career Centres and e2i are accessible via any Family Service Centre (FSC) or Social Service Office (SSO).

## KEY GOVERNMENT SCHEMES

### Workforce Singapore (WSG)
Workforce Singapore is the statutory board under the Ministry of Manpower (MOM) responsible for developing the Singapore workforce, facilitating job placement, and managing sector-specific manpower strategies. WSG operates a network of **Careers Connect** centres offering career coaching, job matching, and employment facilitation.

- **Careers Connect hotline:** 6883 5885
- **Walk-in centres:** Careers Connect @ Paya Lebar, Careers Connect @ One-North, Careers Connect @ Woodlands
- **Website:** wsg.gov.sg

### Employment and Employability Institute (e2i)
e2i is NTUC's employment and employability institute, operating as a key implementing partner of WSG. e2i provides job placement services, employment facilitation for union members and non-members, and career transition programmes.

- **Phone:** 6474 0606
- **Walk-in:** 80 Jurong East Street 21, #04-01 Devan Nair Institute, S609607
- **Website:** e2i.com.sg

### SkillsFuture Credit
All Singapore Citizens aged 25 and above receive SkillsFuture Credit to offset approved course fees, encouraging lifelong learning and skills upgrading. The credit can be applied to thousands of approved courses on the MySkillsFuture portal.

- **Opening credit:** $500 (for citizens aged 25+); additional top-ups for mid-career workers aged 40+
- **Portal:** myskillsfuture.gov.sg
- **Administered by:** SkillsFuture Singapore (SSG)

### SkillsFuture for Mid-Career / CareersFuture SG
A suite of programmes including mid-career pathways, career conversion programmes (CCPs), and placement-and-train initiatives targeting Singaporeans aged 40 and above who are looking to pivot sectors or reskill.

- **Career Conversion Programmes (CCPs):** Employer-funded salary support during retraining for job-matched hires
- **Website:** skillsfuture.gov.sg/initiatives/mid-career

### SGUnited Jobs and Skills
Ongoing framework coordinating government, employers, and training providers to expand job and training opportunities, particularly for job seekers affected by economic disruption. Encompasses traineeships, skills programmes, and job placement across sectors.

- **Access via:** MySkillsFuture portal or Careers Connect centres

### RISE (Reintegration into Society through Employment)
A Yellow Ribbon Singapore programme that supports ex-offenders and their families with employment facilitation, skills training, and employer engagement as part of reintegration from prison or supervision.

- **See section:** Ex-Offender Reintegration (below)
- **Phone (Yellow Ribbon Singapore):** 6297 8820

Persons released from prison or on supervision orders face significant barriers to employment. The Yellow Ribbon ecosystem coordinates government and VWO efforts to support reintegration.

Yellow Ribbon Singapore is a government initiative under the Singapore Prison Service that coordinates rehabilitation and reintegration support, including employment facilitation, community outreach, and the RISE programme.

## ELDERLY EMPLOYMENT

Singapore has a rapidly ageing population, and several initiatives support older workers (aged 55+) in remaining employed or re-entering the workforce.

### Senior Worker Support Package
Government package that includes employer incentives to hire and retain senior workers, and wage offsets for raising retirement and re-employment ages.

- **Administered by:** Ministry of Manpower (MOM) / WSG
- **Website:** mom.gov.sg/employment-practices/re-employment

### NTUC Senior Workers Action Network (SWAN)
NTUC's union-based network supporting senior workers on employment matters including re-employment rights, skills upgrading, and dispute resolution.

- **Contact via NTUC:** 6213 8008 | **Website:** ntuc.org.sg

### Silver Workforce (SG Enable / Workforce Singapore)
Targeted programme for senior workers with disabilities or those who are harder-to-place, providing additional placement and job coaching support.

- **Enquiries via WSG:** 6883 5885 or SG Enable: 1800-858-8585

### Silver Spring (NTUC Silver Spring @ NTUC Club)
Employment facilitation for seniors aged 50+, including placement in selected job roles within NTUC and partner employers, and skills upgrading initiatives.

- **Phone:** 6213 8000 | **Website:** silverspring.com.sg

## FINANCIAL SUPPORT DURING JOB SEARCH

Individuals and families may need financial assistance while a job seeker is between jobs or undergoing retraining. The following schemes bridge income gaps.

### ComCare Short-to-Medium-Term Assistance (SMTA)
Temporary monthly cash assistance for lower-income individuals who are unemployed or earning insufficient income, including those actively job searching.

- **Eligibility:** Gross monthly household income ≤$1,900 or per capita income ≤$650; Singapore Citizen or PR
- **Support:** Monthly cash, utility and rental assistance; case management; job referral to WSG/e2i
- **Apply at:** Nearest Social Service Office (SSO) or call **1800-222-0000**

### ComCare Interim Assistance
Short-term emergency financial assistance for households facing sudden income loss or crisis, providing immediate relief while a full SMTA assessment is completed.

- **Apply at:** SSO or call **1800-222-0000**

### CDC Vouchers (FY2025)
All Singaporean households receive CDC Vouchers regardless of income. Can be used at supermarkets and heartland merchants to offset daily living expenses.

- **Amount:** $800 total across 2025–2026
- **Claim at:** go.gov.sg/cdcv (Singpass login required)

### SkillsFuture Jobseeker Support Scheme
Provides monthly financial support to involuntarily unemployed Singapore Citizens actively seeking work and participating in approved job search or training activities. Announced from 2024 onwards as part of broader workforce safety net.

- **Enquiries:** 6883 5885 (WSG) or myskillsfuture.gov.sg

### Self-Help Group Emergency Assistance
CDAC, Mendaki, SINDA, and the Eurasian Association each provide emergency financial assistance to their respective communities during periods of unemployment.
