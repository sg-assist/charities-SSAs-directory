*You have just learned your child may have developmental needs. This guide walks you from "something seems off" to long-term planning. Breathe. Singapore has a solid, subsidised ecosystem, and you are not the first parent on this path.*

---

## STEP 1 — RECOGNISING THE RED FLAGS (Birth to Age 6)

Use your **Child Health Booklet**. It lists 7 recommended developmental screening touchpoints at well-baby visits. **Speak up** — parents are usually right.

### Red flags by age

| Age | Red flags |
|-----|-----------|
| **By 8 weeks** | No social smile |
| **By 6 months** | Cannot sit with support; hands still tightly fisted; no babbling; poor eye contact |
| **By 12 months** | No pincer grasp; not responding to name; no gestures (waving, pointing) |
| **By 18 months** | Not pointing to show interest; fewer than 6 words; no pretend play |
| **By 2 years** | Not combining 2 words; **loss of previously acquired skills (regression is always a red flag)** |
| **By 3 years** | Speech hard to understand; cannot follow simple instructions; no interest in other children |
| **Any age** | Regression in any domain, persistent repetitive/restrictive behaviours |

Autism and speech/language delays together account for 53–58% of developmental concerns in Singapore preschoolers.

---

## STEP 2 — FIRST MEDICAL TOUCHPOINT (SCREENING)

### Subsidised public pathway

1. **Polyclinic GP or paediatrician** — share concerns; they refer onward
2. **KKH Department of Child Development (DCD)** — largest unit in Singapore. Referral required. *Wait time typically several months.*
3. **NUH Child Development Unit (CDU)** — alternative public option; waits often shorter
4. **KKH Keat Hong Satellite Clinic** — sometimes shorter queues

Subsidised consultation ~$30–$60/visit for citizens (private rates without subsidy: several hundred dollars).

### Private pathway (faster, costlier)

Private developmental paediatricians — 1–4 weeks wait. Expect **$300–$700 per consult**, plus **$1,500–$4,000** for a full diagnostic assessment (ADOS-2, WPPSI, ADI-R).

---

## STEP 3 — FORMAL ASSESSMENT

A full workup usually involves:

- **Developmental paediatrician** — diagnosis, medical workup, co-morbidities
- **Clinical/educational psychologist** — cognitive (IQ), adaptive (Vineland), autism-specific (ADOS-2, ADI-R), ADHD scales
- **Speech-language pathologist** — expressive/receptive language, social communication
- **Occupational therapist** — sensory, motor, self-help skills
- **Physiotherapist** — gross motor, tone, gait

The assessment produces a written report — **keep multiple copies**. You'll need it for EIPIC, SPED admission, FDW Levy Concession, SNTC, and more.

---

## STEP 4 — CALL SG ENABLE (Your Single Front Door)

**SG Enable is the national focal agency for disability in Singapore.** Before you try to figure out every scheme yourself, call them.

- **Infoline: 1800-8585-885** (Mon–Fri 9am–5:30pm)
- Email: contactus@sgenable.sg
- **Enabling Guide portal:** enablingguide.sg
- **Enabling Village:** 20 Lengkok Bahru — walk in for face-to-face help

SG Enable can: explain schemes, help complete applications (EIPIC, ATF, FDW concession, SNTC), connect you to SSAs, handle adult-disability transitions later.

## STEP 5 — EARLY INTERVENTION (Under Age 7)

### Option A: EIPIC (moderate to high needs)

**Early Intervention Programme for Infants and Children** — funded by MSF/ECDA, delivered by 12+ SSAs. **Referral routed through SG Enable after diagnosis.**

**Fees (post-subsidy, means-tested):**
- Singapore Citizens: **$5–$430/month**
- Permanent Residents: **$20–$1,270/month**

**Major providers:**

| Provider | Specialty | Locations |
|----------|-----------|-----------|
| **AWWA** | Broad disabilities | Kim Keat, Fernvale, Fernvale Woods |
| **Rainbow Centre** | Intellectual/multiple | Margaret Drive, Yishun Park, Admiral Hill |
| **SPD Building Bridges** | Physical/multiple | Bedok, Kembangan, Tampines |
| **CPAS** | Cerebral palsy, complex physical | Multiple |
| **Fei Yue** | General EI | Jurong East, Bukit Batok, Wellington Circle, Upper Thomson |
| **Canossian** | Hearing impairment (primary) | Canossaville |
| **ARC WeCAN** | Autism-specific | Ang Mo Kio |
| **THKMC** | General EI | Ang Mo Kio, Choa Chu Kang, Tampines, Woodlands |

Also: **EIPIC Under-2s** (earlier entry) and **EIPIC-Care** (higher-support).

### Option B: DS-LS (mild needs, in mainstream preschool)

**Development Support-Learning Support Programme** — Learning Support Educators (LSEds) work in the child's own preschool. For children with mild needs in K1–K2.

- **Access:** Speak to your preschool; they initiate the referral
- **Covers:** speech & language, social skills, motor skills, behaviour, early literacy
- **Fees:** DS $5–$290/month; LS $5–$130/month (means-tested)

**DS-Plus** exists for children needing more than DS-LS but less than full EIPIC.

## STEP 6 — SCHOOL-AGE DECISION (From Age 7)

### Pathway A: Mainstream School with Learning Support

- **Allied Educators (Learning & Behavioural Support) — AED(LBS)** in every MOE school
- Supports mild conditions: dyslexia, ADHD, mild ASD, mild sensory/physical impairments
- Additional: School Counsellors, Special Educational Needs Officers (SENOs), exam access arrangements (extra time, reader, prompter)

### Pathway B: SPED Schools (22+ schools, 12 SSAs)

**Fee update — from mid-2025:** monthly SPED fees for Singapore Citizens are now **max $90/month** (from $150); more than half pay **$70 or less**.

| Disability | Schools |
|-----------|---------|
| **Autism** | Pathlight (AWWA), Eden (ARC), St Andrew's Autism School |
| **Mild Intellectual Disability** | APSN Katong/Tanglin/Chaoyang, Metta School, Grace Orchard |
| **Moderate–Severe Intellectual** | MINDS (Lee Kong Chian, Towner, Woodlands, Fernvale Gardens) |
| **Multiple Disabilities** | CPAS, Rainbow Centre (Margaret Drive, Yishun Park, Admiral Hill), AWWA School |
| **Hearing Impairment** | Canossian School, Mayflower Special School |
| **Visual + Hearing** | Lighthouse School |

**Admission:**
- Standardised SPED application form submitted to your **first-choice school**
- Required: formal diagnosis, assessment reports, **School Screening Assessment**
- **Pathlight** (most competitive): age 7–18, formal ASD diagnosis, adequate non-verbal cognition, verbal/literacy readiness, self-help skills
- **Singapore Citizens have absolute priority**; PRs only after all SC applications

## STEP 7 — FINANCIAL ASSISTANCE (Stack These)

1. **Assistive Technology Fund (ATF)** — up to **90% subsidy**, **$40,000 lifetime cap**. From Jan 2026, PCHI threshold raised from $2,600 to **$4,800**. Applications submitted by an assessing therapist
2. **Special Needs Savings Scheme (SNSS)** — CPF-based. Nominate CPF savings to pay **monthly ($250 min)** to a child with permanent disability after parent's death. No minimum balance, no fees. Administered by SNTC. Child must have attended SPED or need help with ≥1 ADL
3. **Medifund** — safety-net for healthcare bills. Via hospital medical social worker
4. **MediSave** — usable for outpatient treatment of chronic conditions including ASD and ADHD (Chronic Disease Management Programme)
5. **KidSTART / ComCare** — for lower-income families
6. **Disability-specific bursaries** from SSAs (e.g., DAS for its own programmes)

---

## STEP 8 — CAREGIVER SUPPORT

- **Migrant Domestic Worker Levy Concession:** **$60/month** instead of $300. Eligible: person with disability needing help with ≥1 ADL. Up to 2 concessions per household
- **Home Caregiving Grant (HCG):** $200/month (rising to $600 from April 2026). Via AIC
- **Respite care:** via EIPIC centres, SPED schools, Rainbow Centre / AWWA respite
- **Caregiver training:** CAL, SG Enable, AIC, disability-specific SSAs
- **Support groups:** Down Syndrome Association, Autism Association, SPARK, DAS Parent Support, CPAS Family Support, Rare Disease Society

## STEP 10 — TRANSITION TO ADULTHOOD (Post-18)

Planning from age 14–16 is ideal.

- **Open employment** with SG Enable support — job coaches, Enabling Academy, School-to-Work Transition Programme
- **Sheltered Workshops (SW)** — training plus structured work. Providers: SPD, MINDS, THK, AWWA. **SW2W** programme transitions capable trainees to open jobs
- **Day Activity Centres (DAC)** — for higher-support adults. Life skills, community integration, caregiver respite
- **Enabling Skills for Life Programme (ESLP)** — integrates DAC + SW with flexible pathways
- **Capacity expansion:** +520 DAC and +360 SW places rolling out 2026–2027
- **Adult Disability Homes** — MINDSville, THKMC Home, Bishan Home, AWWA Home

Apply for adult services through SG Enable's Adult Disability Services application (Enabling Guide).

## STEP 11 — LEGAL & ESTATE PLANNING (Start Early)

Cover all four instruments:

1. **Special Needs Trust Company (SNTC)** — the **only non-profit trust company** for PWDs in Singapore, jointly backed by MSF and NCSS. Receives gifts under wills, insurance, CPF nominations, and disburses funds as a Care Plan. Fees heavily subsidised — designed for ordinary families, not only the wealthy
2. **Special Needs Savings Scheme (SNSS)** — the CPF component (see Step 7), administered by SNTC
3. **Will** — names guardians, directs assets. Should coordinate with SNTC trust and CPF nominations
4. **Lasting Power of Attorney (LPA)** — ensures that if **you** (the parent) become mentally incapacitated, a trusted donee can make decisions, protecting your child's ongoing care

**Sequence:** Make LPA for yourself → set up SNTC Trust → make SNSS nomination → update Will and CPF nominations to align.

## THE MINIMUM VIABLE ACTION PLAN (NEXT 4 WEEKS)

1. **Week 1:** Call **SG Enable Infoline 1800-8585-885**. Get a GP/polyclinic referral to **KKH DCD or NUH CDU**
2. **Week 2:** If public wait is long and you can afford it, book a private developmental paediatrician in parallel (do not cancel the public referral — you will need subsidised follow-up)
3. **Week 3:** Talk to your child's preschool about **DS-LS**. Tour 1–2 EIPIC centres near you (AWWA, Rainbow Centre, SPD, Fei Yue) so you are ready to enrol once the report is in hand
4. **Week 4:** Apply for **MDW Levy Concession** if you employ a helper. Start reading the **Enabling Guide** (enablingguide.sg) — the single most useful resource

Then, over the next months: diagnosis → EIPIC enrolment → financial schemes (ATF, HCG) → SNTC/SNSS/LPA/Will → caregiver support group.
