## THE JOURNEY IN ONE PARAGRAPH

From the first signs of forgetfulness, visit a GP or polyclinic for screening and referral to a hospital memory clinic (TTSH, KTPH, IMH, SingHealth, NUH). Immediately after diagnosis: make a Lasting Power of Attorney (LPA) with the Office of the Public Guardian, register for CARA membership with Dementia Singapore, and accept a CREST-PDS link-up for case management. As symptoms progress, enrol in Family of Wisdom, Memories Café, and New Horizon Centre day care; join a caregiver support group; apply for the Home Caregiving Grant and CareShield Life payouts through AIC. Add home care when day care is insufficient. When 24-hour care is needed, apply through AIC for a dementia nursing home (Apex Harmony Lodge or a subvented home with a dementia ward). At the advanced stage, request Programme Dignity or hospice care — so that the journey that began with "Mum is forgetting things" ends with dignity and peace.

## STAGE 1: "Something Is Wrong" — Diagnosis

### Where to start
GP or polyclinic for screening (AMT/MMSE/MoCA), then referral to a specialist memory clinic. Mild Cognitive Impairment converts to dementia at 10–15% per year — early follow-up matters.

### Memory clinics at public hospitals

| Clinic | Hospital | Contact |
|--------|----------|---------|
| **TTSH Cognition & Memory Disorders Service (CMDS)** | Tan Tock Seng Hospital, 11 Jalan Tan Tock Seng | 6357 7095 |
| **KTPH Memory Clinic** | Khoo Teck Puat Hospital, 90 Yishun Central | 6555 8000 |
| **IMH Memory Clinic** | Institute of Mental Health, 10 Buangkok View, Block 7 | 6389 2200 |
| **SingHealth Duke-NUS Centre for Memory & Cognitive Disorders** | Covers SGH, CGH, SKH, NNI | Via SingHealth |
| **NUH Memory, Ageing & Cognition Centre (MACC)** | National University Hospital | Via NUH |

**Diagnosis typically involves:** history, cognitive testing (MMSE/MoCA/ACE-R), blood screen (TFT, B12, folate), MRI/CT. Young-onset or atypical cases may add FDG-PET or amyloid PET.

> **Practical tip:** Ask for a letter of diagnosis — you will need it for the LPA, HCG application, and nursing home means testing.

## STAGE 2: Community Mental Health Support

### CREST (Community Resource, Engagement & Support Team)

Neighbourhood-level outreach teams operated by VWOs (Care Corner, AWWA, NTUC Health, Fei Yue, Filos, TOUCH, Allkin).

- **CREST-PDS (Post-Diagnostic Support)** — Structured case management for the first 1.5 years after diagnosis
- **CREST-Caregivers** — For caregivers at risk of anxiety, burnout, depression

**How to access:** Ask your memory clinic to refer, or call the Dementia Helpline **6377 0700**.

### COMIT (Community Intervention Team)

Higher-tier service providing psychotherapy, CBT, psychoeducation, psychosocial interventions.

**Access:** Referral via polyclinic, hospital, GP, or self-referral through AIC Link **1800-650-6060**.

## STAGE 3: Lasting Power of Attorney (LPA) — DO THIS NOW

Critical: LPA must be made **while the person still has mental capacity**. Once capacity is lost, families must apply for Deputyship through the Family Justice Courts — expensive and slow.

### Process

1. **Choose form:** LPA Form 1 (standard, free registration for SG citizens — permanent) or LPA Form 2 (customised, requires lawyer)
2. **Get certified:** By a certificate issuer (doctor, lawyer, or psychiatrist) who confirms the donor understands the document
3. **Submit** to the Office of the Public Guardian (OPG)
4. **Processing:** Typically 3 weeks

### Office of the Public Guardian (OPG)
- Phone: **1800-111-2222**
- Email: opg@msf.gov.sg
- Under the Mental Capacity Act (2008, effective 2010), administered by MSF

Launched under **Forget Us Not** initiative by Lien Foundation and KTPH. Six original DFCs:

2. **MacPherson**
3. **Hong Kah North** — pilot for Go-To Points / safe return
4. **Bedok**
5. **Queenstown**
6. **Fengshan**

Later additions: Bukit Batok East, Woodlands, and more.

**What DFCs offer:**
- Dementia Friends training for residents and shopkeepers
- **Go-To Points** — shops, clinics, community centres trained to help seniors who get lost
- Safe-return protocols
- **Dementia Friends mobile app** (launched October 2018) — report lost persons with dementia

## STAGE 8: Residential / Nursing Home Care

### Apex Harmony Lodge — The first purpose-built dementia home

Opened 1999. First residential home in Singapore designed specifically for dementia, around an open-plan "village" concept.

- Address: 10 Pasir Ris Walk, S518240
- Phone: **6585 2265**
- Admission: **Only via AIC referral**, after means testing
- Website: apexharmony.org.sg

### Other dementia-capable nursing homes

Most MOH-subvented nursing homes now have dedicated dementia wards: All Saints Home, Econ, Lee Ah Mooi, Orange Valley, St Luke's, Peacehaven, Ren Ci, Kwong Wai Shiu, THK, Sunlove, Villa Francis.

### Admission process (AIC)

1. Get a hospital or GP referral letter, or go through a Medical Social Worker during an inpatient stay
2. AIC conducts care-needs assessment and means testing
3. AIC matches to available nursing home based on medical needs, location, waitlist
4. **Costs:** Start from ~$3,900/month unsubsidised; up to 75% subsidy for eligible households; MediSave usable at $200/month

**AIC Link: 1800-650-6060**

### Respite options

- **Day respite** at NHC / AWWA / NTUC Health dementia day care
- **Short-stay residential respite** — AIC can arrange 1–4 weeks at a nursing home
- **Home-based respite (Eldersit)** — Dementia Singapore provides Eldersit free for caregivers attending support sessions
- **CAL Caregivers-to-Caregivers (C2C) 12-week programme** — free, via cal.org.sg

### Caregivers Training Grant (CTG)

- **$400/year** per care recipient for approved caregiver training courses
- Apply via AIC
- Covers dementia care, tube feeding, wound care, transferring

### Home Caregiving Grant (HCG)

- **Increasing to $600/month from April 2026** (from $400)
- For care recipients with permanent moderate disability (needs help with ≥3 ADLs)
- Usable for eldercare services, foreign domestic worker, consumables
- Apply at supportgowhere.life.gov.sg or via AIC

## STAGE 11: Advance Care Planning

### While capacity is preserved

- **LPA Form 1** — via OPG online (eLPA) or paper form
- **Will** — make or update with a lawyer (capacity must be assessed if already diagnosed)
- **Advance Care Planning (ACP)** — Discuss future wishes with a trained facilitator. Documented and accessible to treating teams. Available at all public hospitals, many polyclinics, and via AIC's Living Matters programme
- **Advance Medical Directive (AMD)** — Legal document refusing extraordinary life-sustaining treatment if terminally ill

### After capacity is lost (no LPA in place)

Apply for **Deputyship** via Family Justice Courts. Simplified track for dementia since 2023 — faster, no lawyer needed for straightforward cases.

## STAGE 12: Advanced Dementia and End-of-Life

### Recognising advanced stage

FAST stage 7 / GDS 7: loss of speech to <6 intelligible words, inability to walk/sit/smile, recurrent infections (pneumonia), pressure sores, dysphagia, weight loss.

### Palliative care services

| Service | Details | Contact |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **Programme Dignity for Advanced Dementia** | Home palliative care for housebound advanced dementia (Dover Park + TTSH, since 2014) | Via TTSH Geriatric Medicine or Dover Park |
| **Dover Park Hospice** | Inpatient (50 beds), day, home hospice. 10 Jalan Tan Tock Seng | **6500 7272** |
| **Assisi Hospice** | 85 inpatient beds including dementia palliative ward, day hospice, home care. 832 Thomson Rd | **6347 6446** |
| **HCA Hospice Care** | Singapore's largest home hospice (since 1989). 12 Jalan Tan Tock Seng | **6251 2561** |

### Financial support at end of life

- Home hospice and day hospice are **typically free or heavily subsidised** for citizens/PRs
- Inpatient hospice: means-tested, subsidies up to 75%
- **MediSave-500 / MediShield Life** can be used for palliative care
- **Compassionate Care Fund** for families facing financial hardship

### After death

- Hospice or GP issues the Certificate of Cause of Death (CCOD)
- Register death at any Neighbourhood Police Post (24 hours)
- Bereavement counselling is free from Dementia Singapore, Dover Park, Assisi, HCA
